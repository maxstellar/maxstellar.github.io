Recently, I was a reviewer for a Hack Club program called [Jackpot](https://jackpot.hackclub.com/). As cool as the program was, the reviews were... depressing. Emma herself said [in a Slack message](https://hackclub.slack.com/archives/C0ADEGZL5HD/p1775945588784089):

> [A]round 80% of the reviews, was spent rejecting entire projects due to clear, full use of AI without minimal human contribution. Many of the submitted websites look almost identical, heavily vibe-coded, and in some cases include scripts designed to automatically track or generate hours.

I remember getting in a call with @owais and going through reviews, only for us to send every other project to each other, asking "is this AI again?" And this isn't even an issue unique to Jackpot. Although it was particularly apparent with Jackpot, it's also known that Flavortown faces issues with AI slop as well, to the point where Hack Clubbers joke about it quite often on the Slack.

This isn't to say there are no good projects coming to YSWSes anymore. In fact, quite the opposite. During my time reviewing for Hack Club: The Game and Jackpot, I saw so many creative, high-effort, and amazing projects submitted, and it truly made me incredibly proud of our community (if not a little bit disappointed in myself for being so unaccomplished). But the truth is, a lot of slop is being submitted, and some of it even makes it past our review process. So, what can we do to reduce this?
