It's March 13th. I'm scrolling #ysws, when I notice that people are mentioning something - an internship? At Hack Club?
Keep in mind that this is early into his Hack Club career Max, and so I was exactly 2 months into my return to Hack Club. I had no idea about anything to do with HC - not its culture, its history, anything. To me, this was completely new.
So, I go ahead and I look into it. Looks like I'd spend a month in Vermont at HQ, running my own YSWS, working with talented people, and meeting like-minded teens. Okay. I am so sold. It is now my life's mission to become a Hack Club intern.

The application is to design a website for your YSWS idea, and obviously, to pitch your YSWS idea. Almost perfectly, I was already working on a YSWS called "Telephone," based off of the popular YouTube series where a number of game developers pass a game between themselves, with absolutely zero communication - and the idea was basically the same, except with a bunch of teens.

Here's how I described the basic loop in my pitch:

1. Get secretly assigned a team (or up to 3 teams) 🤫
2. On your turn, work on your game over the course of 4 days! 🧩
3. Ship your contributions + receive small prizes! 🛫
4. We'll pass your game to the next person 🤝
5. After your team's game is done, finally meet your team + discuss to receive a final prize! 🎉

After a bit of contemplating this idea, I begin having doubts. It feels flimsy, hard to execute well. And so, I make the smartest decision I've had in a while, and I ask for advice. At this point, I'd gotten to know a few talented people, and even some working at Hack Club HQ, and so I ask them for their opinions and thoughts. I even got some advice from Max Wofford himself! All signs seemed to point to the same thing - this idea was not going to land me an internship.

So, I begin working on a new YSWS idea; I think about it in the morning when I wake up, in the car on the way to school, in my Trigonometry class, during lunch, while watching Jet Lag, and before I go to bed at night. I take a lot of things into account while brainstorming: Why would people do my YSWS over other ones? Who would my audience be? Most importantly, would Deven and Max and HQ (the people reviewing internship applications) like my idea?

## a short except on ysws design

So you want to design a YSWS. Cool! There are a lot of things to take into account - so strap in.
First off, there are two (main) reasons that people would want to do a non-flagship YSWS, and thus YSWS can usually be assigned to two categories.

1. The idea is cool
   The thing you're actually shipping is really cool. Think #hackpad, where participants are likely doing it because they genuinely want to learn how to create a macropad, or #saycheese, where people would love to challenge themselves to fit an entire program inside of a QR code. These types of programs drive engaged learning, creativity, or both!
2. The prizes are good
   The prize for your YSWS is something that people want. There are both good and bad sides to this type of YSWS; this can be something that drives creativity and hacker value (such as an IRL hackathon/event, or a hackable piece of tech that not only pulls WGs, but inspires further learning and hacking) or it can just be something completely mundane, but popular, so that many people will want it (which I think, is a little lazy.)
